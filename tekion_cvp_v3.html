Customer Value Review
The Customer Value Plan is a living account document — not a meeting artifact, not a reporting template. It defines what a dealer is trying to achieve, where they are in their Tekion journey, and what the CVM and CVA will do next to move them forward. This document activates Sections 1 through 3. Sections 4 through 7 will be introduced as operational infrastructure and data capability mature.

Dealer goals and success criteria
What this dealer is trying to achieve. CVM-defined from relationship context.
CVM owns ►
Why this section matters
This is the section that separates a reactive account manager from a value manager. If you don't know what success looks like for this dealer — in their terms — every conversation defaults to support and status updates. Defining goals and success criteria upfront gives the CVM something to sell, something to measure, and something to bring back to the dealer as evidence of progress. A CVP without this section is an account record, not a plan.

Session guidance — CVMs
If success criteria aren't yet defined with this dealer, document your best hypothesis based on what you know from the relationship. The goal is to surface the gap so you can close it — not to produce a polished answer. A blank field with an explanation is more useful than a guess dressed up as a fact.
3
Maturity and adoption status
CVA-owned input. Grounds the plan in observed behavior, not relationship optimism.
CVA contributes ►
Why this section matters
The maturity tier tells you where a dealer is. The adoption data tells you why. Without this section, the CVP is a relationship document — with it, it becomes a diagnostic. The CVA's job here is to translate platform data into a clear picture: what's working, what's stalled, and what the next move should be. This is also what connects the account plan to the CVR conversation — the adoption data is the evidence you bring to the table.

Risk flags and renewal readiness
Early warning signals. Ensures no renewal catches the team off guard.
Draft — Phase 2 ►
Why this section matters
Renewals are won or lost long before the renewal date. This section exists so that risk is visible, named, and owned — not discovered 30 days before expiration. It also creates the discipline of distinguishing between a relationship that feels fine and an account that is actually healthy. The CVM's read on sentiment is not the same as a health signal. This section holds both.

Expansion opportunities
Modules and capabilities not yet activated. Requires Sales alignment before actioning.
Draft — Phase 2 ►
Why this section matters
Expansion is the downstream result of doing everything in Sections 1 through 6 well. A dealer who has realized value, has an active champion, and trusts the CVM relationship is a dealer who is ready to expand. This section documents where that opportunity exists and connects the adoption data from Section 3 to a growth conversation. It also defines the handoff point between CS and Sales — a boundary that needs to be clean before this section can be actionable.

The CVP is updated when something changes — not on a fixed calendar. Any of the following should trigger a review and update:
